The Evolution of the Oscars

A statistical retrospective of how the Oscars have changed over the years.

By Tiana McGee

November 30, 2023

Credit:https://www.cornucopia-events.co.uk/the-oscar-academy-awards/

In the past 23 years, the prestigious awards ceremony known as The Oscars has witnessed a remarkable evolution, honoring outstanding achievements across various categories. With over 1,878 individuals recognized, the annual awards ceremony is a beacon of accomplishment for many. However, since its inception, much like the industry itself, The Oscars hasn’t always been an institution that welcomed all. In the age of Barbenhiegmier, women have made their way to the forefront, but is Hollywood giving them their desserts when it comes to awards?

In this data of the last 23 years the overall male nominees and winners dominate over females.

While the number of male nominations is still higher than females throughout the years, the gap has been getting smaller.

In 2000, there were around five more male winners compared to female. However, in 2023, that difference shrunk to around two.

The gender breakdown unveils that the gap narrows even with male nominees consistently outnumbering females. Even in 2023, we aren’t there yet. This is about more than not winning according to the Women In Film organization in Los Angeles in Variety Magazine, “An Academy Award is more than a gold statue; it’s a career accelerator that can lead to continued work and increased compensation.” Right now, women are still being held back instead of celebrated equally in an industry that belongs to everyone.
